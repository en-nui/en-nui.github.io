About

I’m a PhD Candidate in Evolution, Ecology, and Behavior with Irene Newton at Indiana University. As part of my training, I’m affiliated with the NSF Institute for Genomics in Microbial Systems (GEMS).

Before my PhD, I graduated from UMass Amherst in 2015 and returned back to my home in South Carolina. I drove tractors and conducted plant pathology experiments in a joint appointment with the USDA-ARS and Clemson University. I also spent a few years cooking in a classic, French bistro.

I then left the USDA to go conduct fieldwork in Chernobyl, Ukraine in order to explore the impact of radionuclide contamination on fungal biodiversity as a Fulbright Fellow. During that time, I learned to leverage science as a tool of international diplomacy and community building.

Now broke from a life in research (and a year of traveling), I leased my body and soul to a biotechnology startup, where I helped expand the company’s sequencing pipeline from 96 to >4,000 samples per week. I also developed assays that helped secure several crucial IP and patents.

Much of my life’s joy is centered around three B’s: Biking, baking, and being (in my garden).
